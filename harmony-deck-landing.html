Three steps, in order

Each stage needs the one before it — you can't harmonize a part that hasn't been isolated yet, or mix a part that hasn't been generated.

01

Split

Drop in a recording. Mid/side processing pulls your vocal from the mix using center-channel extraction.

02

Harmonize

The key gets detected automatically. Each part is your isolated vocal, pitch-shifted to the right interval — not a synth voice.

03

Mix & export

Fade, mute, solo, and tone-shape every part on the rack. Bounce stems or a finished master as WAV.

What's under the hood

A real DSP pipeline, not a black box — every stage is a documented technique you can inspect in the source.

Mid/side vocal isolation

Center-channel extraction pulls a lead vocal from a stereo mix without needing separate stems.

Autocorrelation key detection

Pitch-tracks your take and matches it against major/minor key profiles — or set the key by hand.

Granular harmony shifting

Each part is generated by resynthesizing your own recorded performance at the chosen interval.

Live tone shaping

Warm Alto, Bright Soprano, Airy Choir, and more — on-the-fly EQ character per channel.

Stem & master export

Bounce every part as an individual WAV stem, or a mixed-down master, straight from the browser.

Optional cloud AI engine

Plug in a self-hosted DiffSinger model for fully AI-sung parts from lyrics and notes.

It feels like a mixer, not a settings page

Faders, knobs, mute and solo on every channel — the same tactile rack you'd find in a real vocal booth, rendered entirely in the browser.
